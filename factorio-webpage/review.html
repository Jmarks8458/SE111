Review

Jesse Marks, 1/30/2024, ★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Factorio is the ultimate unpaid labor game. You are forced to use critical thinking and logic just like you use when building a program, and not a light one at that. You do all of this while fighting off endless hordes of bugs that just don't ever seem to stop comming, just like actual programming! Despite all of this sounding a bit like just a job, it's a game. And this game is absolutely amazing. There is a reason the players of this game call it 'Cracktorio' because once your hooked you won't notice until hours later when you finally look back at the clock in astonishment. You can create amazing efficient factories with provide you with a thrill few other things can. You can create spagetti factories, which you will, on your first try if you don't know to do otherwise. You can play with trains and signals making sure goods get from point A to point B efficiently and with as few delays as possible. Most Factorio players consider the end game goal as really a mid game, because once you launch your rocket into space you stand on an edge. You can either stand back and appreciate your efforts and decide you have done enough and close the game. Or you can do what the rest of us do once we launch that first rocket and ask if we built it efficiently enough, and then you start working on creating the best, biggest, most efficient factory possible. Suddenly its as if nothing had happened, no rocket was launched, the factory is the same, the bottlenecks are still there, THE FACTORY MUST GROW. This game is amazing and I would highly recommend it to any person who thinks they might be intersted because if you like the game you'll love the game, and even if you don't like it you can still appreaciate the awe that comes with building even the simplest of assembly lines. Good luck engineers and remember, the factory must grow.
